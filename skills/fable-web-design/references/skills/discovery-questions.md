# Discovery Questions: Kickoff Question Protocol

Run a structured question round at the start of new or ambiguous design work.
Asking good questions is the single biggest lever for design quality.

## Phase 1: Read What's Already Attached
Before asking anything, read every attached resource: codebases, screenshots,
brand guides, linked design systems, the user's brief.

## Phase 2: Decide Whether to Ask

**Ask when:** work is new/ambiguous, output/audience/fidelity unclear,
user hasn't specified variations, task spans multiple nontrivial dimensions.

**Skip when:** user gave everything needed, small tweak, explicit scope.

A question is justified by the design impact of its answer, not by your
uncertainty.

## Phase 3: Build the Question Set

### Always-ask questions:
1. **Design context**: "Is there a UI kit, design system, codebase, brand
   guide, or screenshots I should match?"
2. **Variations**: How many overall design variations? What axes to vary on?
   Variations of specific elements?
3. **Novelty**: By-the-book or novel ideas, or mix?
4. **Tweaks**: What should be tweakable in the final design?
5. **Focus axis**: What do you care about most — flows, copy, or visuals?

### Problem-specific questions (3-6):

**For a landing page:** What action do you want users to take? Primary
persona? Competitors or references (positively or negatively)? Mobile-first
or desktop-first?

**For an interactive prototype:** What flow/screens? Hi-fi or mid-fi? Device
frame? Goal state? Sample data?

**For a brand or aesthetic:** Mood/tone in 3 adjectives? Brands you admire?
Anything off-limits? Industry/context?

## Phase 4: Format
Bundle into one form. Use multiple choice when possible. Order so most
important come first. Keep titles short.

## Phase 5: End the Turn
Let the user answer. Read every answer before starting to design.

## Phase 6: Confirm and Proceed
Briefly recap choices that affect the design. Note any surprises. Then
proceed to the appropriate building skill and execute autonomously.

## Anti-patterns
- Don't skip asking
- Don't ask everything (cap around 10-15 questions)
- Don't ask one at a time across multiple turns
- Don't ask about details you can derive from attached assets
- Don't ask to be safe
