# Frontend Aesthetic Direction: Commit to a Look

Once the brief is clear, commit to an aesthetic direction before wireframing.
This skill prevents the "let me explore a few options" trap where every
iteration starts from scratch.

## The Direction Statement

Write a one-paragraph direction statement covering:
1. **Visual metaphor** — what the design is "like" (not what it is)
2. **Color approach** — palette type, dominant tone, accent strategy
3. **Typography** — font personality, size scale approach, hierarchy method
4. **Spatial strategy** — density, breathing room, grid discipline
5. **Motion philosophy** — what moves, when, and why
6. **What this is NOT** — explicit rejections of obvious alternatives

## Direction Quality Check

Before moving to wireframes, verify:
- The direction is specific enough that two designers would produce similar outcomes
- It includes explicit "don'ts" that prevent drift back to defaults
- It names at least one reference (site, designer, movement) that captures the target feel
- It explains why the chosen references match this brief specifically

## Pattern to Avoid

"Make it modern and clean" is not a direction. It's the absence of one.
Every default AI aesthetic starts with these three adjectives. Push past them.

## From Direction to Wireframe

Once direction is committed:
1. Lock the direction — revisits cost more than they save
2. Generate 3+ wireframe variations within the locked direction
3. Each variation tests a different structural choice (not a different color)
