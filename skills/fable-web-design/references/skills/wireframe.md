# Wireframe: Low-Fi Variations

Generate 3+ low-fidelity wireframe variations before any hi-fi work.
The purpose is to fail cheap — structural problems found at wireframe stage
cost nothing to fix.

## Purpose of Multiple Variations

One wireframe is a proposal. Three wireframes is a decision. Each variation
should test a different structural choice:
- Variation A: conventional, safe layout
- Variation B: bold structure, known risk
- Variation C: hybrid or unexpected

All within the locked aesthetic direction.

## Wireframe Discipline

- No color decisions at wireframe stage. Grayscale only.
- No typography decisions beyond hierarchy levels (H1, H2, body).
- No content polish. Placeholder text that shows structure, not lorem ipsum.
- Annotations, not explanations. Mark spacing, hierarchy, and flow with
  brief notes, not paragraphs.

## User Review Gate

Present wireframes, get feedback on structure before any hi-fi work.
Structural changes at hi-fi stage are 10x more expensive.

## From Wireframe to Prototype

Once structure is approved:
1. Lock the structure
2. Move to prototype with real content in the approved layout
3. Apply the aesthetic direction (color, typography, spacing)
